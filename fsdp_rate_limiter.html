Rate Limiter

FSDP allocates AllGather buffers in a producer stream (NCCL); forward/backward computation runs in a consumer stream (default). The caching allocator assigns blocks per-stream — a block allocated for the producer stream cannot be reused by the consumer stream, even after the AllGather finishes. If the CPU runs ahead and issues too many AllGathers, the producer stream claims too much of the total GPU memory, leaving insufficient room for the consumer to allocate activations — forcing a costly cudaMalloc retry (blocking cudaFrees to reset allocator state). The rate limiter caps in-flight AllGathers to 2, preventing over-allocation.  Paper § 3.4.
